The global Climate Finance Accelerator (CFA) is a four-year technical assistance programme funded by the U.K. Department for Energy Security and Net Zero and contracted by PricewaterhouseCoopers. DAI, as a subcontractor, is delivering the first two phases of the CFA activities in Pakistan by convening a wide variety of stakeholders, including project developers, banks and other finance providers, and government representatives.  
 
Following a successful first phase, DAI is currently delivering Phase II of the CFA in Pakistan. This phase builds on our track record by continuing to convene a wide variety of stakeholders including project developers, financial institutions, and government representatives to bridge the gap between low-carbon projects and investment.

## Select Results (CFA I Legacy) 
 
CFA II builds upon the strong foundation laid by the first cohort: 
 
* **Portfolio Growth**: The programme has supported 22 projects to date. 
* **Investment Mobilization**: These projects represent a collective investment mobilization value of approximately US$40 million, advancing them toward commercial deal making. 
* **Community Impact**: These projects demonstrated clear potential to benefit local communities through pollution abatement, job creation, and improved access to energy. 
* **Investment Readiness**: Support included rigorous needs assessments and financial, technical, and social inclusion analysis, ensuring projects were positioned to attract substantial investment. 
* **Notable Alumni**: Previous cohorts include industry leaders and innovators such as National Foods, Daewoo Express, Shams Power, Davaam Life, and Concept Loop. 

## Global Context 
 
The CFA operates in 10countries, including Pakistan, Colombia, South Africa, and Vietnam. Globally, more than 200 projects have graduated from the programme, unlocking more than $450 million in investment so far.
